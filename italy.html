Italy

Italy is a dream destination for people world-wide, whether it’s kids fresh out of high school looking to study abroad, newlyweds looking for a romantic honeymoon, or retirees looking to travel in their golden years. Its extensive history, beautiful sceneries, and rich culture make Italy a popular vacation spot.

However, no country is perfect; Italy has its fair share of issues. Italy began with a dictatorship under Benito Mussolini, which nearly destroyed the country. After WWII, the government put in place was the democratic republic system, which helped the country revive its economy and its people.

Italy has been a member of NATO sine the end of the 20th century. While Italy may be plagued by the same issues that haunt any country (ex. crime, unemployment, pollution, etc.), it is still a country full of life and history, making it a desired vacation destination and a beautiful place to live.

Geography

Italy is famous for being a “boot-shaped” peninsula located in the Eastern hemisphere of the globe. The country extends far into the Mediterranean Sea, which is another one of its attractions; the Mediterranean Sea offers gorgeous beaches and is full of marine life that make it an intriguing attraction.

Location

Italy is located in Southern Europe, northeast of Tunisia. It extends far out into the Mediterranean Sea. While the islands Sardinia and Sicily are not directly connected to the main country itself, they are still considered a part of Italy.

Land Boundaries

Italy has a total area of 301,340 sqkm, making it 73rd of the list of countries by size. Italy borders six other countries, with a varying amount of distance making up each boundary line.

Environment

Italy’s climate varies based on geographic location. Italy is hot and dry in the south where the North is more mountainous. Italy’s mean elevation is 538, with the highest point being Mont Blanc de Courymayeur at 4,748 m and the lowest point being the Mediterranean Sea at 0 m.

People and Society

Italy is home to a large population of 62,390,364 people, not including the tourists and visitors that travel there yearly. The majority of residents are Italian, with small numbers of people also being German, French, and Slovene. This means that the official language of Italy is Italian, with small areas speaking other languages such as German and French, depending on the population.

With Italy’s nearness to the Vatican City, it means that 80.8% of the population is Roman Catholic. However, about 4.9% of people are Muslim and 13.4% are unaffiliated with a religion.

Ages of Population

The median age of the population is 46.5 years; the age is increased due to the death rate being 10.7 deaths/1,000 population, which isn’t very high compared to other countries. The population is growing at a rate of 0.09%; the majority of the population comes from people who migrate later in life. The age structure of Italy is as follows:

  1. 0-14 years:13.45%
  2. 15-24 years: 9.61%
  3. 25-54 years: 40.86%
  4. 55-64 years: 14%
  5. 65 years and over: 22.08%

Infants and Birth

The birthrate is 8.37 births/1,000 population, with the infant mortality rate at 3.14 deaths/1,000 live births. Life expectancy at birth is 82.67 years, with females expected to live longer.

The average age for first-time mothers is about 31.3 years. Approximately 1.47 children is born per woman.

Urbanization

The population of Italy is really distributed along the country. However, urban centers like Rome, Milan, and Naples attract denser populations. There is a 0.27% rate of urbanization annually, bringing the Urban Population up to 71.3% of the total population. With Rome being the capital of Italy, it has the largest urban population of 4.278 million people.

Health and Safety

Drinking water has been improved for 100% of the population and is accessible to the entire population. Sanitation facilities are also accessible to 98.8% of the population.

About 0.2% of the population is living with HIV/AIDS, which is about 140,000 people. However, deaths due to HIV/AIDS is less than 1,000. The current health expenditure of Italy is 8.7%, with physician density at 3.98 physicians/1,000 populations.

The major infectious disease affecting Italy for the past two years is Covid-19 (which is the case for most countries globally). There have been about 214.4 deaths per 100,000 deaths due to Covid-19.

Education

Education expenditure is at 4% of GDP, which puts Italy at 98th on the list of countries and education. 99.2% of the population meets literacy requirements (which are reading and writing). School life expectancy is 16 years on average (but females are expected to be in school for longer at 17 years.

Government

Italy (known as Italia to locals) has a parliamentary republic government. The capital of Italy is Rome. Compared to Washington, DC, Italy is 6 hours ahead in time. Italy gained independence as its own country onMarch 17th, 1861. Politics are divided into numerous parties: Democratic Party, Forza Italia, Brothers of Italy, Free and Equal, More Europe, and Popular Civic List.

Constitution

The constitution of Italy was last enacted Dec. 22nd, 1947, and was entered into force Jan. 1st, 1948. To get an amendment added to the constitution, it must first be proposed by both houses of Parliament. It then must be approved by most of each house on the second vote. The constitution of Italy has been amended numerous times, with the last added in 2020.

Citizenship

Unlike the United States, a person cannot be an Italian citizen by birth; one parent must be a citizen of Italy. Refugees must be residents for at least 5 years before becoming a citizen. However, EU nationals only must achieve residency of 4 years.

Government Branches

The chief of state is President Sergio Mattarella, who has been in office since Feb. 2015. Mario Draghi is the prime minister, and he oversees appointing Council of Ministers.

The president is elected by indirectly by the electoral college for a term of 7 years. The prime minister is then appointed by the president.

In the legislative branch, there are 321 seats; each member then serves for life. The Chamber of Deputies has 630 seats, and they serve for 5 years.

The Supreme Court of Cassation is the highest court of the Judicial Branch. The court has 54 different justices and 288 judges. These judges may serve for life.
